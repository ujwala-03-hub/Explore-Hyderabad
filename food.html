Hyderabad’s culinary scene is a feast for the senses. The iconic Hyderabadi Biryani, slow-cooked with fragrant basmati rice, tender meat, and rich spices, is a must-try. Irani cafés serve up strong chai and buttery Osmania biscuits, perfect for a cozy break. Street food lovers can indulge in spicy mirchi bajji, tangy pani puri, and sweet double ka meetha. Whether you're dining in a palace-style restaurant or grabbing a bite from a roadside stall, every flavor tells a story.

Famous Foods

Hyderabadi Biryani

Hyderabadi Biryani is a legendary dish that embodies the rich culinary heritage of Hyderabad. Originating in the royal kitchens of the Nizams, this aromatic rice preparation blends Mughlai and Deccani flavors into a single, unforgettable experience.

Irani Chai

Irani Chai is a creamy, slow-brewed tea that’s become a beloved ritual in Hyderabad’s old-world cafés. Unlike masala chai, it’s not about bold spices—it’s about richness and depth.

Double ka Meetha

Double ka Meetha is a rich and indulgent Hyderabadi dessert that transforms simple bread into a royal treat. The name comes from “double roti,” a local term for milk bread that puffs up when baked.

Haleem

Hyderabadi Haleem is a rich, slow-cooked stew that blends the flavors of wheat, lentils, meat, and aromatic spices into a velvety, nourishing dish. Originating from the Middle Eastern dish Harees, Haleem was brought to Hyderabad by Arab soldiers and later adapted to suit the local palate. Today, it stands as one of the city’s most iconic foods, especially during the holy month of Ramadan.

Pani puri

The puri must be eaten immediately after filling to preserve its crunch. The balance of flavors is key—too much pani makes it soggy, too little makes it dry. The ideal pani puri is a one-bite wonder: crisp shell, cool liquid, warm filling, and a flavor explosion.

Meals

Meals are known for their rich flavors, spicy curries, and diverse vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes, making them a culinary delight

Mutton Kheema Samosa

Mutton Kheema Samosa is a savory Indian snack featuring a crispy pastry shell filled with spiced minced mutton (kheema), peas, and aromatic herbs, deep-fried to golden perfection.

Chicken Shawarma

Chicken Shawarma is a Middle Eastern street food superstar that’s gone global. It’s all about marinated chicken, slow-roasted or grilled until juicy and charred, then sliced thin and wrapped in warm pita or flatbread with creamy sauces and crunchy veggies.

Pathar Ka Gosht

Pathar-ka-Gosht is a popular lamb dish, especially prepared in Hyderabad, Telangana, India. [1] This dish is prepared with mutton by heating it on a wide stone, on a flame. [2] The spices are added once the meat pieces are heated and served with onions and other ingredients.

Paya Soup

Paya Soup is a rich, hearty dish made from the trotters (legs) of goat or lamb, simmered slowly to extract deep flavor and nutrients. It's especially popular in South Asian cuisine and is often enjoyed during colder months or as a nourishing remedy for joint and bone health.

Boti Kebab

Boti Kebab is a bold and flavorful dish from the heart of Indian cuisine, especially popular in Lucknowi and Mughlai traditions . It features chunks of meat—usually lamb, mutton, or chicken—marinated in a rich blend of spices and grilled to smoky perfection.

Shahtoot Malai

Shahtoot Malai, where “Shahtoot” translates to mulberry and “Malai” to cream, stands as a symbol of culinary sophistication in Indian cuisine. This dessert captures the essence of luxurious dining, intertwining the velvety texture of cream with the burst of flavor from fresh mulberries

Qubani ka Meetha

Qubani ka Meetha, a traditional Hyderabadi dessert made from dried apricots, is known for its rich, sweet-tangy flavor and jam-like consistency.

Falooda

Falooda is a popular Ice cream dessert made with vermicelli, jelly, rose syrup, sabja seeds, milk and ice cream.

Nalli Gosht Biryani

Nalli Gosht Biryani is a sumptuous and aromatic dish that hails from the rich culinary heritage of Indian cuisine. This biryani variant is renowned for its tender pieces of lamb shank (nalli gosht) cooked to perfection with fragrant spices and basmati rice.
