Landmarks of Hyderabad

Historic places of hyderabad

Charminar

The Charminar, built in 1591, is the most iconic landmark of Hyderabad. It stands in the heart of the old city and is surrounded by lively bazaars.

Golconda Fort

Golconda Fort is a historic citadel and was once the capital of the medieval Golconda Sultanate. It is known for its acoustic wonders and majestic architecture.

Chowmahalla Palace

Chowmahalla Palace was the seat of the Asaf Jahi dynasty and the official residence of the Nizams of Hyderabad. It is renowned for its grandeur and elegance.

Qutb Shahi Tombs

The Qutb Shahi Tombs are located near Golconda Fort and house the tombs of the rulers of the Qutb Shahi dynasty. These tombs showcase Persian and Indian architectural styles.

Paigah Tombs

Paigah Tombs are famous for their intricate marble carvings and unique architectural styles, blending Mughal, Persian, and Deccan influences.

Bansilalpet Stepwell

The recently restored Bansilalpet Stepwell is a heritage water conservation structure that highlights Hyderabad’s sustainable architecture from centuries ago.

Birla Mandir

Birla Mandir is a beautiful temple made of white marble, dedicated to Lord Venkateswara. It is located on Naubat Pahad hill and offers panoramic views of the city.

Mecca Masjid

One of the largest mosques in India, Mecca Masjid was built during the reign of Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah and can accommodate thousands of worshippers.

Salar Jung Museum

The Salar Jung Museum houses one of the largest one-man collections of artifacts in the world, featuring art, antiques, and treasures from across the globe.

Tank Bund & Buddha Statue

The Tank Bund road offers a beautiful view of Hussain Sagar Lake, with the majestic Buddha statue standing tall in the middle of the lake.

Shilparamam

Shilparamam is an arts and crafts village in Hyderabad showcasing traditional crafts, cultural events, and folk performances.

Nehru Zoological Park

Spread across 380 acres, the Nehru Zoological Park is home to a wide variety of animals, birds, and reptiles, making it a popular family attraction.

Ramoji Film City

Ramoji Film City is the world’s largest film studio complex, where visitors can experience film sets, entertainment shows, and theme parks.

Lumbini Park

Located near Hussain Sagar Lake, Lumbini Park is famous for its musical fountain shows, boat rides, and gardens.

Falaknuma Palace

Falaknuma Palace, now a luxury hotel managed by Taj, was once the residence of the Nizam. Its lavish interiors and 101-seat dining hall are legendary.
